Quick Summary for Parents & Athletes (English)

  • No alcohol, tobacco, or drugs at any time during the season.
  • No stealing or abuse of school/athletic property.
  • Take care of all gear. Lost or damaged equipment must be paid for before awards or next sport.
  • No profanity or vulgar language in the program.
  • Be on time and present for practices, meetings, trips, school and competitions.
  • Show respect to coaches, teachers, teammates, opponents, officials and fans.
  • Maintain good conduct on campus, on trips and in the community.
  • Stay academically eligible under LUSD and CIF rules.
  • Follow grooming and team-dress standards set by coaches.
  • Travel with the team to and from contests unless released directly to a parent/guardian.
  • Follow all training rules, team policies and varsity letter requirements set by each coach.
  • Understand that violations can lead to suspension or dismissal from the team.

Suspension and dismissal decisions follow the process outlined by the LHS Athletic Department, including possible review by an Athletic Review Board.

Resumen Rápido para Padres y Atletas (Español)

  • No se permite el uso o posesión de alcohol, tabaco o drogas durante la temporada.
  • No se permite robar ni dañar la propiedad de la escuela o del equipo.
  • El equipo y uniforme deben cuidarse; si se pierden o dañan, deben pagarse antes de recibir premios.
  • No se permite el lenguaje vulgar u ofensivo.
  • Se requiere puntualidad y asistencia regular a prácticas, reuniones, viajes, competencias y escuela.
  • Respeto obligatorio hacia entrenadores, maestros, compañeros, rivales, oficiales y aficionados.
  • Buena conducta en la escuela, en los viajes y en la comunidad.
  • El estudiante debe mantener la elegibilidad académica según LUSD y CIF.
  • Seguir las reglas de presentación personal y uniforme establecidas por los entrenadores.
  • Viajar con el equipo a y desde las competencias, salvo que el entrenador libere al atleta con su padre/madre.
  • Seguir todas las reglas de entrenamiento y políticas del equipo, incluyendo los requisitos para letra varsity.
  • Las violaciones pueden resultar en suspensión o expulsión del equipo.

Las decisiones de suspensión o expulsión siguen el proceso del Departamento Atlético de LHS, con posible revisión por un Comité Atlético.

Additional Wrestling Program Expectations

  • Closed Practices: Daily practices are closed to spectators. Parents are welcome at meets and tournaments, not at regular practice.
  • Contact Windows: Please avoid contacting coaches in the 30 minutes before or after practice and competitions, unless it is an emergency.
  • Best Way to Reach Coaches:
    • For most questions, send a text message or email first.
    • Use phone calls only for time-sensitive or emergency situations.
    • For bigger concerns, request a meeting time by text or email so we can talk calmly and privately.
  • Respectful Conversations: Post-match coaching and debates on the sideline are not part of our standard. We will gladly review concerns at a scheduled time.

These expectations protect practice time, athlete focus and clear communication for everyone.

SCHOOL POLICY Rev 8/20 ATHLETIC DEPARTMENT LOMPOC HIGH SCHOOL 515 West College Ave, Lompoc, California 93436, Telephone (805) 742-3073 STANDARD ATHLETIC AGREEMENT TO: All Athletes and their parents FROM: The Athletic Department and Lompoc High School Administration You have indicated that you would like to participate in the interscholastic sports program at Lompoc High School. We are happy that you are interested in this program for we believe that participation in a well conducted athletic program has real value for you. We believe that the following training rules and regulations will lead to better conditioning, better spirit and better achievement. Since you participate in team sports entirely on a voluntary basis, you do so with the understanding that these rules will be observed for the entire season, which begins with the first day of practice and concludes with the last regularly scheduled contest or the last C.I.F. Play-off contest in which the team participates. 1. Possession or use of ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES in any form is PROHIBITED. 2. Possession or use of TOBACCO PRODUCTS in any form is PROHIBITED. 3. Possession or use of any SUBSTANCE OF ABUSE of substances commonly identified as “DRUGS” is PROHIBITED. 4. STEALING (of Lompoc High School equipment, of opponent school’s equipment, from teammates, etc.) or ABUSE OF SCHOOL PROPERTY IS PROHIBITED. 5. RESPONSIBILITY is assumed for the maintenance of equipment and accessories issued for sport participation throughout the year. All repairs beyond normal wear, and if the equipment is LOST or DAMAGED, 100% of the value of the equipment must be PAID FOR BEFORE an athlete receives any awards or is ELIGIBLE to compete in a subsequent sport. Value is the depreciated value as stated on the latest equipment appraisal. It is a portion of the replacement cost. 6. PROFANITY is PROHIBITED. Vulgar and obscene language has no place in athletics. 7. PUNCTUALITY and REGULAR ATTENDANCE is REQUIRED. Absences from practices, team meetings, conditioning sessions, trips, competitions or school, for reasons other than the EXCUSED ABSENCE listed in the Parent/Student Handbook, must be authorized in advance through the Athletic Director’s Office or by an Administrator. However, these authorized absences will be limited to three periods on the day of a contest or the day after a contest. PUNCTUALITY also applies to all practices, meetings, conditioning sessions, trips, competition, and school attendance. My parents and I have read and understand the above rules and regulations. We agree that they are reasonable and as a Lompoc High School athlete, I agree to abide by them, and also to abide by any additional rules and team policies of my particular sport. Signature of Athlete ____________________________ Date ___________ Signature of Parent or Guardian __________________ Date ___________ NAME OF SPORT(S) Rev 8/20 8. RESPECT for coaches, teachers, fellow athletes, opponents, officials and fans is REQUIRED! Fair play and good sportsmanship is expected and required for athletes at all times. 9. GOOD CONDUCT is REQUIRED! Good citizenship is expected and required of athletes at all times—on campus, on trips, and in the community. It is important that athletes SET A GOOD EXAMPLE! 10. ELIGIBILITY is REQUIRED. The Lompoc Unified School District AND C.I.F. standards for eligibility must be met at all times. 11. GOOD GROOMING is REQUIRED. All athletes are expected to be clean and neat; many teams also require UNIFORM dress at various times; each coach may set the minimum standards for a particular team. 12. TRAVEL with the team is REQUIRED. All athletes are required to travel with the team to and from athletic contests or practices. A coach MAY release an athlete to travel with a PARENT or GUARDIAN only! 13. Adherence to TRAINING RULES and TEAM POLICIES set by a particular coach for a specific sport is REQUIRED. 14. These additional rules and policies, including the REQUIREMENTS FOR RECEIVING A VARSITY LETTER, must be written and distributed to all team members by the coach. Disciplinary action for violations of the Athletic Agreement are left to the discretion of the coaching staff of the specific sport involved. Athletes may be SUSPENDED from athletic teams for violations of this Athletic Agreement. During the suspension, the athlete may be asked to practice, but not to travel or compete; or the athlete may be asked to stay away from practice, trips, or competition for a definite period of time. Suspension is an intermediate form of discipline and violation of the terms of suspension may lead to dismissal. Athletes may be DISMISSED from athletic teams for violations of the Athletic Agreement and barred from ANY athletic participation for a period of four weeks or the remainder of the season, whichever is greater. Athletes WILL BE DISMISSED from athletic teams for violations of Article 1, Article 2, or Article 3 of page one of this Athletic Agreement. ALL dismissals are subject to review by an Athletic Review Board upon written request of the athlete involved. The Board will consist of any three head coaches (including the coach whose team is involved), the Athletic Director, and the Principal. It shall be within the power of the Board to determine whether a dismissal might be mitigated to become a suspension. “Guidelines” for the Review Board process are available in the Athletic Office. An athlete must FINISH THE SEASON to be eligible for an award, including all regularly scheduled contests, as well as all C.I.F. play-off contests. If an athlete “quits” a team or is “dismissed”, he or she FORFEITS ALL AWARDS; an athlete who is “suspended” during a team’s season may or may not receive awards, depending upon meeting requirements for lettering, etc. An athlete who quits one sport is NOT ELIGIBLE for practicing or participation in another sport until the sport he or she has quit has FINISHED its entire season. An athlete who is “cut” at the beginning of the season IS immediately eligible to join another team.
