Parent Meeting — Coach Outline

Purpose: Quiet script for you during the meeting. Parents never see this page. Use it as a calm roadmap while you talk.

Tip: Keep this open on your phone or laptop while parents use the public QR page.

Opening — Calm & Confident

Coach Cue:

“Thanks for making time again tonight. Last week was quick — tonight is about clarity and direction. The culture is already in the room; my job is to protect it.”

“This meeting is about people — our kids, our families, and our standard.”

Pause, look around the room. Slow your breathing. Let the silence work for you.

1) Team Policy & Conduct

Key points to hit:

  • Attendance is mandatory unless cleared directly with Coach.
  • “If you’re early, you’re on time. If you’re on time, you’re late.”
  • Grades = eligibility = trust.
  • Practice discipline: hygiene, effort, focus, teamwork.
  • Adults-only meeting tonight for clear communication.

Optional line: “Your kids are learning grown-up standards in a safe place. We hold them accountable here so life hits softer later.”

2) Schedule & Travel

Key points:

  • Walk through boys/girls calendars at a high level.
  • January is heavy — mid-season grind. Ask for patience and support.
  • Overnights: clear behavior and supervision standards — no exceptions.
  • Team stays together; parents coordinate with Coach, not independently.
  • Volunteers for snacks, transportation, and chaperoning are appreciated.
3) Weight & Hydration

Key points:

  • CIF hydration rules — no crash cutting, no extreme dehydration.
  • Focus on long-term health: sleep, real food, and recovery.
  • Let parents know you will not push unsafe cuts, even under pressure.

Optional line: “I’d rather lose a match than risk a kid’s health. We’ll be competitive the right way.”

4) Team Parents & Support

Key points:

  • Introduce Maya as Boys Team Parent; Girls Team Parent TBD.
  • Clarify: they handle logistics, reminders, sign-ups — not decisions.
  • Ask for unified sideline energy — one voice, one standard.

Optional line: “When kids look into the stands, they should see one united team — not mixed messages.”

5) Communication (Para-Comms Loop)

Key points:

  • “Fast, clear, direct” — text or call Coach, not through athletes.
  • Team Parents: reminders only, not middlemen for disputes.
  • No sideline coaching, no post-match arguments with officials.
  • Mention simple bilingual portal/QR as backup for updates.

Keep this simple. The system will grow later; tonight is about trust and clarity.

6) Culture Message

Mini story (optional):

Share one short example: athlete staying late to clean mats, helping a teammate, or fighting through a tough practice without quitting.

Close with: “These are the moments that build champions long before medals. Thank you for backing that up at home.”

7) Closing

Key lines:

  • “We’re ahead of schedule. We just keep stacking days.”
  • “Thank you for trusting me with your kids. I don’t take it lightly.”
  • “If you have private concerns, I’ll stay in the hallway after we wrap.”
Reminder: This page is for Coach only — do not share QR or URL with parents.
