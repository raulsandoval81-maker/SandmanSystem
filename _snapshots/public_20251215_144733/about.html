Programs

The Sandman System™ offers structured, XP-driven training for youth, teens, and young adults. Foundry 8 builds the foundation. Foundry 4 deepens that journey into advanced combat, leadership, and lifestyle.

Every lane shares the same core: combat first, character always. The difference is the age, pace, and responsibility level expected inside each program.

Core Training Lanes

Each Foundry lane carries built-in pillars of Strength and Honor. These aren’t separate “add-ons” — they’re woven into every Combat practice and XP cycle across Foundry 8 and Foundry 4. Athletes train their body, mind, and habits as part of the same grind.

Youth · Zero2Hero

Foundry 8 — Youth Combat

[ Youth training image · wrestling / boxing / kickboxing ]

Roughly 2nd–8th grade. A youth combat path that teaches respect, discipline, and confidence — with XP-based progress from Shadow all the way to Hero.

  • Wrestling foundations
  • Boxing & striking fundamentals
  • Kickboxing movement and control
  • Light competition exposure when athletes are ready

Teens & Young Adults · Path2Legend

Foundry 4 — Combat

[ Teen / young adult combat image ]

High school and older athletes sharpen skill, power, and discipline. This lane is for those ready to be pushed, held accountable, and trusted with example status in the room.

  • Wrestling and mat control
  • Boxing and striking combinations
  • Kickboxing and pressure drills
  • Competition prep and mindset work
  • Future lanes: advanced grappling & MMA-style foundations

Advanced · Lifestyle & Leadership

Foundry 4 — Lifestyle & Leadership

[ Leadership / captain huddle / classroom image ]

For advanced athletes, captains, and coaches in training. This lane extends beyond competition into how athletes live, lead, and serve in their families, schools, and communities.

  • Lifestyle & wellness habits
  • Leadership, mentoring, and example-setting
  • Coach-path development for future staff
  • Future branches: Seniors, Family, and early youth lanes
